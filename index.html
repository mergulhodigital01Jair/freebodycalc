About HealthCalc Hub

HealthCalc Hub is a free collection of online health and fitness calculators. Our tools use established scientific formulas to give you useful estimates about your body and health needs. All calculations happen instantly in your browser — we never store your data.

BMI Calculator

Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by the square of your height in meters. While BMI doesn't account for muscle mass or body composition, it remains one of the most widely used screening tools for identifying potential weight-related health concerns.

Calorie Calculator (TDEE)

Our calorie calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, widely regarded as the most accurate formula for estimating Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR). Your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is then calculated by multiplying BMR by your activity factor, giving you the number of calories you need to maintain, lose, or gain weight.

Water Intake Calculator

Proper hydration is essential for virtually every function in the body, from regulating temperature to lubricating joints and supporting digestion. The recommended daily water intake varies based on weight, activity level, and climate. Our calculator provides a personalized estimate using current hydration research guidelines.

Sleep Cycle Calculator

The human sleep cycle lasts approximately 90 minutes and includes both REM and non-REM stages. Waking up mid-cycle often causes grogginess (sleep inertia), while waking between cycles feels more natural. Our sleep calculator helps you find optimal bedtimes or wake-up times aligned with your natural sleep cycles.

Body Fat Calculator

Our body fat calculator uses the US Navy circumference method, which estimates body fat percentage based on height, neck, and waist measurements (and hip for women). While DEXA scans remain the gold standard for body composition analysis, this method provides a reasonable estimate without equipment.

Macro Calculator

Macronutrients — protein, carbohydrates, and fat — are the three main types of nutrients that provide energy. The optimal balance depends on your goal. Fat loss typically benefits from higher protein, muscle gain from higher carbohydrates, and ketogenic diets from very high fat. Our macro calculator adjusts the ratios based on your selected goal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate are these calculators?
Our calculators use validated scientific formulas and provide useful estimates for the general population. Individual results may vary based on factors like muscle mass, medical conditions, and genetics. For medical decisions, always consult a healthcare professional.
Is my data stored or shared?
No. All calculations run entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Nothing you enter is ever sent to our servers, stored, or shared with third parties.
What formula does the calorie calculator use?
We use the Mifflin-St Jeor equation (1990), which is currently the most recommended formula for estimating BMR. It is considered more accurate than the older Harris-Benedict equation for most adults.
How many sleep cycles should I aim for per night?
Most adults need 5-6 complete sleep cycles per night, which corresponds to 7.5–9 hours of sleep. Our calculator shows wake-up times based on 4, 5, and 6 complete cycles so you can choose based on your schedule.
